<h3 id="toc0"><span>Properties that apply to all field types</span></h3>
<h4 id="toc1"><span><a name="label"></a> The 'label' property</span></h4>
<p>If you specify a 'label' property then the field gets that text in the left column, or before the field for joined fields. If you do not specify a label then the field has an empty space in the left column, or is squashed up after the previous field, for joined fields. For example:</p>
<div class="code">
<pre>
<code>[[form]]
fields:
  address-line-1:
    label: Address
    width: 30
  address-line-2:
    width: 30
  address-line-3:
    width: 30
[[/form]]</code>
</pre></div>
<h4 id="toc2"><span><a name="join"></a> The 'join' property</span></h4>
<p>If you specify 'join: true' then the field is placed after the previous field, if any. This property has no effect if the field is the first in the form. For example:</p>
<div class="code">
<pre>
<code>[[form]]
fields:
  city:
    label: City
    width: 20
  postcode:
    label: Postcode
    width: 8
    join: true
[[/form]]</code>
</pre></div>
<h4 id="toc3"><span><a name="before"></a>The 'before' property</span></h4>
<p>Provides a string of plain text that displays before the field value</p>
<div class="code">
<pre>
<code>[[form]]
fields:
  phone:
    label: Phone
    width: 10
    before: +(1)
[[/form]]</code>
</pre></div>
<h4 id="toc4"><span><a name="after"></a> The 'after' property</span></h4>
<p>Provides a string of plain text that displays after the field value</p>
<div class="code">
<pre>
<code>[[form]]
fields:
  speed:
    label: Car speed
    width: 4
    after: kph
[[/form]]</code>
</pre></div>
