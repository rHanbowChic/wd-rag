<p>Paragraphs are separated by two new lines. One new line produces a… new line.</p>
<div class="code">
<pre>
<code>First paragraph. Lorem ipsum dolor sit amet, consectetuer adipiscing elit.

Second paragraph. Aenean a libero. Vestibulum adipiscing, felis ac faucibus imperdiet, erat lacus accumsan neque, vitae nonummy lorem pede ac elit.
Just a new line.
Another new line.</code>
</pre></div>
<p>First paragraph. Lorem ipsum dolor sit amet, consectetuer adipiscing elit.</p>
<p>Second paragraph. Aenean a libero. Vestibulum adipiscing, felis ac faucibus imperdiet, erat lacus accumsan neque, vitae nonummy lorem pede ac elit.<br/>
Just a new line.<br/>
Another new line.</p>
<p>Line-Break:<br/>
there is a special character used for line-break at the end of a line which does not start a new paragraph and is very useful in tables or divs where it is sometimes needed:<br/>
it contains a "space" and "underscore" (and then "enter" ) to insert such "\n" in the code and start at the next line.</p>
<p>The difference is not easy to show ( because spaces + enter are rendered also in Code blocks to nearly one line):</p>
<div class="code">
<pre>
<code>Only 5x space+ Enter:

Last line after 5 x enter 

With special Line-Break: _
 _
 _
 _
 _
Last line after 5 x special line-Break</code>
</pre></div>
<br/>
Only 5x space+ Enter:
<p>Last line after 5 x enter</p>
<p>With special Line-Break:<br/>
<br/>
<br/>
<br/>
<br/>
Last line after 5 x special line-Break</p>
<p>Do not forget that spaces alone or empty lines are rendered to one line!</p>
