<h1 id="toc0"><span>One account - access many sites</span></h1>
<p>At the WikiDot.com network all the sites share the same User accounts. It means that having one account guarantees the same User identity at all the sites.</p>
<h1 id="toc1"><span>User status within a site</span></h1>
<h2 id="toc2"><span>Anonymous User</span></h2>
<p>Some sites may allow anonymous users (i.e. these who do not use a valid WikiDot account - are not logged in) to modify content and use discussion forum. In any such case the visitor's IP address will be stored and publicly visible.</p>
<h2 id="toc3"><span>Registered Users</span></h2>
<p>Registered Users are these who have and use a valid Wikidot account but are not necessarily a member of a given site. Some sites allow such Users to modify content and use forum.</p>
<h2 id="toc4"><span>Members of the site</span></h2>
<p>Members of the particular site are these who through some process joined the site. One can become a Member of a site by</p>
<ul>
<li>applying to Site Administrators (if enabled)</li>
<li>by providing a valid <em>membership password</em> (if enabled)</li>
<li>by accepting an invitation from a Site Administrator</li>
</ul>
<p>Some sites allow content modification and forum postings only to its Members. In fact this is the default ;-)</p>
<h3 id="toc5"><span>Site Administrators</span></h3>
<p>Users who have all possible permissions within a site. A User who creates a new site also becomes an Administrator of this site. Other site Members can be also given Administrator roles.</p>
<h3 id="toc6"><span>Site Moderators</span></h3>
<p>Users who have certain permissions to modify content but can not access site settings. Moderators can have any of the two roles</p>
<ul>
<li>Page Moderator - can modify content pages</li>
<li>Forum Moderators - can modify (edit, delete) forum threads and posts.</li>
</ul>
<p>Site Moderators are given their roles by Site Administrators.</p>
<h1 id="toc7"><span>WikiDot.com Superusers</span></h1>
<p>There is also a group of Superusers which most possibly belong to Wikidot.com staff. They can do a lot.</p>
